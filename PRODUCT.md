# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary visitors are luxury and design buyers — people who already care about objects with presence, not people shopping for features. They arrive with time and attention, often on a large display, looking for whether this piece feels inevitable. Context is quiet and considered: late evening, a private tab, not a lunch-break scan.

Their job is to understand the craft well enough to want a private relationship with the atelier, not to complete a self-serve checkout.

## Product Purpose

An immersive web experience that presents a luxury timepiece as a singular sculptural encounter. The page exists so a visitor can feel precision, material, and human making through light and motion — then choose a high-touch next step.

Success is when the visitor can describe the object’s character after ten seconds, and when “request a private viewing” feels like the natural continuation of that encounter rather than a sales form.

## Positioning

The watch as living sculpture — not a catalog entry, but a singular object you meet in space.

## Brand Personality

Warm, craft, human. Voice is intimate and exact: atelier language, not luxury-ad gloss. Emotion goals are quiet awe, tactile desire, and trust in the maker’s hand. Restraint over spectacle; presence over pitch.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS and startup landing patterns — feature grids, stock gradients, “modern product” chrome, and conversion-optimized section stacks that treat the object like software.

## Design Principles

1. **Object first.** Every layout decision subordinates UI chrome to the timepiece in space. If the interface competes with the model, the interface loses.
2. **Warm craft, not cold gallery.** Luxury here is human and material — wood, metal, skin-warm light — not sterile white cubes or ice-blue tech.
3. **Show the making.** Understanding craft is the job; surface story, process, and material truth before any hard ask.
4. **Invitation over pressure.** The primary path is a private viewing; the soft path is deeper craft story. Never countdown, badge, or hard-sell cart energy.
5. **Motion serves the object.** Auto-rotation, parallax, and light shifts exist to reveal form — not to decorate the page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.2 AAA where practical, with AA as the hard floor. Keyboard access to all actions, focus states that remain visible on dark cinematic backgrounds, and text contrast that holds over the 3D scene (solid overlays or scrims as needed). Prefer reduced-motion alternatives: when `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is set, freeze or heavily damp the 3D loop and prefer a high-quality still or subtle crossfade so the experience remains fully usable.
